# IMRAD Structure

The IMRAD (Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion) structure is the most common format for scientific research papers.

## 1. Introduction

- **What was the question?**
- Provide background information on the topic.
- State the research question or hypothesis.
- Briefly outline the experimental approach.

## 2. Methods

- **How did you try to answer it?**
- Describe the experimental design and methodology in detail.
- Provide enough information for other researchers to replicate the study.
- Include information on the materials, procedures, and statistical analysis used.

## 3. Results

- **What did you find?**
- Present the findings of the study in a clear and concise manner.
- Use tables, figures, and graphs to illustrate the data.
- Do not interpret the results in this section.

## 4. Discussion

- **What does it mean?**
- Interpret the results of the study.
- Compare the findings to previous research.
- Discuss the limitations of the study and suggest areas for future research.
- Conclude with a summary of the main findings and their implications.
